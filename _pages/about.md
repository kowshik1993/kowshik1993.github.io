<div style="text-align: justify;">
I'm a graduate student in Cybersecurity at the University of Nebraska at Omaha (UNO), with 8+ years of professional experience in IT infrastructure, network security auditing, and enterprise systems management. My background spans building structured workflows for incident response, compliance auditing, and secure data lifecycle management.

My current research, conducted with the ASTRA Lab at UNO, focuses on **UAV-based acoustic sensing** — specifically drone ego-noise modeling and suppression, and microphone-array direction-of-arrival (DoA) tracking for public-safety event awareness. This work is supported by a **Graduate Research and Creative Activity (GRACA) Award**, funding research into ML-enabled noise reduction and robust acoustic source localization under severe drone ego-noise conditions.
</div>

## Research Interests
<div style="text-align: justify;">
- UAV acoustic sensing and drone ego-noise suppression
- Microphone-array signal processing (beamforming, DoA, source tracking)
- Deep learning for acoustic event detection (e.g., gunshot / distress-call awareness)
- Real-time and edge audio analytics for cyber-physical systems
</div>
## Background
<div style="text-align: justify;">
Before returning to graduate study, I spent over eight years at TSTECH Bangladesh Limited, progressing from Junior IT Officer to Assistant Manager. In that time I led network security audits and forensic readiness assessments across enterprise systems, maintained secure high-availability Oracle-based production systems, and owned ISO 9001 certification and compliance documentation efforts.

I hold a Bachelor of Engineering in Information Science and Engineering from Visvesvaraya Technological University, and I'm currently completing my Master of Science in Cybersecurity at UNO (expected May 2027).
