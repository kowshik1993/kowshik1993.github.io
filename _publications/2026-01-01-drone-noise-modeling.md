This project focuses on suppressing drone propeller (ego-)noise to recover surrounding audio, such as speech or environmental sound, from onboard microphone recordings. The goal is to improve audio quality enough to make downstream tasks — like detecting distress calls or gunshots — reliable even while the drone is hovering or in flight.

This work is part of a broader effort funded by the Graduate Research and Creative Activity (GRACA) Award, supporting UAV-based acoustic monitoring for public-safety event awareness.
